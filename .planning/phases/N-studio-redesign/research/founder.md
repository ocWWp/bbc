# Persona research — Founder (2026)

> Deep-research agent output, 2026 sources.

## Summary
Founder/CEO at a 5-50 person startup is time-starved (~14hr days, ~30% in email). Exec writing is recurring "homework" — too important to delegate, too frequent to enjoy. Workflow is a context-gathering slog *before* any writing. AI is in the first-draft step now but produces generic, voice-flat copy and can't see their actual numbers or history. **Design opportunity: a workspace that pre-loads context and preserves the founder's voice — not another blank box.**

## What they produce (format + cadence)
- **Monthly investor update** — the anchor. 250-750 words at seed/A. Structure: Overview → Highlights → Metrics (cash, runway, KPIs) → Lowlights → Asks → Thanks. Consistency matters more than polish — dropping a previously-reported metric reads as bad news.
- **Board updates/decks** — quarterly, longer-form.
- **Weekly all-hands message** — framing note / talking points.
- **Fundraising narrative** — exec summaries, talking points; cadence spikes during a raise.
- **Strategic memos** — ad hoc, internal alignment.

## Workflow
Gather context (the painful part: pull MRR/runway/headcount, **find last month's update for consistency**, collect team wins/lowlights — ~2hrs/month, mostly copy-paste) → draft (increasingly AI first pass) → edit for voice & truth → send. On-screen: prior update, metrics dashboard, Slack/notes, draft, AI chat — "11-tab sprawl."

## Good vs. mediocre
**Mediocre** = "filing cabinet": dumps numbers, sugarcoats, vague asks. **Good** = "clean dashboard, not a detective novel": brief, scannable for progress/risk/asks in seconds; honest (miss → cause → response, in that order); specific actionable asks. Usually reviewed by no one — sent solo, raising the stakes on getting it right alone.

## Workspace preferences
Hate: re-gathering context every cycle, copy-pasting graphs, hunting for last month's version, a blank doc that doesn't know their numbers. Want: context **pre-loaded** beside the draft, a template that carries forward between cycles, minimal tab-switching.

## Design implications for the Founder Studio
- **Lead with context, not a blank box** — assemble latest metrics + last month's update + team input, visible beside the draft.
- **Template carries forward** — persist structure + metric set between cycles; flag dropped metrics.
- **Voice preservation is the differentiator** — learn prior updates, draft in their voice; output is a first pass they *edit*.
- **Optimize for review, not authoring** — success = a 2hr chore becomes a 15-min review-and-send.
- **Asks-aware** — surface specific asks tied to current challenges.

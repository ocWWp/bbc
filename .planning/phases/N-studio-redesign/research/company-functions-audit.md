# Research — what functions a company needs (the Studio role set)

> Deep-research agent output, 2026 sources. Answers: what's the complete Studio role set?

## Verdict
A 5-50 person startup has ~13 recognizable functions but only ~9 are *distinct* enough to merit a dedicated owner. BBC's "produce a grounded document from company memory" model fits **document-heavy functions** well and **conversational/relationship functions** poorly. **The 5 existing + finance/legal/HR = the correct 8-Studio "clear yes" set.** Product is the strongest future #9.

## "cofounder.com" → cofounder.co
The site the user meant is almost certainly **cofounder.co** — an agent-orchestration platform organized into "agentic departments" (Engineering, Sales & Marketing, Operations, Finance, Customer Support, Design, Legal/Compliance). It ships *roadmaps*, not a template library — but its 7-department structure **independently validates BBC's roadmap**. Actual template *libraries* (First Round Review's 9 magazines, YC Startup Library, Notion "Startup OS" galleries, Business-in-a-Box, Orrick Tech Studio) all cluster around the same recurring document categories.

## The recommended Studio role set

**Clear Yes — ship these (current 5 + 3 planned):**
| Studio | Core output types |
|---|---|
| Founder | investor update, board deck, OKRs/strategy memo, fundraising narrative, all-hands script |
| Marketing | positioning doc, launch plan, content/blog draft, press release, campaign brief |
| Engineering | RFC/design doc, postmortem, technical spec, release notes, runbook |
| Designer | brand guidelines, design brief, UX spec/critique, design system doc |
| Support | help-center article, macro/canned response, FAQ, escalation summary, release-impact note |
| **Finance** *(new)* | financial-model narrative, budget memo, board financials, investor-update numbers, expense/spend policy |
| **Legal** *(new)* | NDA, IP assignment, employment agreement, MSA/service agreement, ToS & privacy policy draft |
| **People/HR** *(new)* | offer letter, handbook section, onboarding plan, performance-review template, comp-band rationale |

**Priority for the new ones:** Finance → Legal → People/HR. (Legal docs are the highest-fit, most-requested template category; HR depends on legal primitives like employment agreements.)

**Maybe Later:**
- **Product Studio (#9)** — PRD, roadmap, spec, release notes, user-research synthesis. Strong document fit; currently absorbed into Founder/Engineering. Add once those feel overloaded.
- **Operations/RevOps** — real demand, fuzzy scope; ship if users ask.

**Doesn't fit the Studio model:** Sales (conversational — at most a thin proposal-drafter assist), Recruiting (sourcing/interviewing is the job, JDs too thin), Data/Analytics (output is dashboards), Partnerships/BD (relationship-driven).

## Sequencing note
HR-as-a-Studio at 5-50 people is slightly ahead of when companies hire an HR *person* (~40-50) — but the *documents* (offer letters, handbooks) are needed from employee #1, so the Studio is justified even before the role exists.

# Persona research — Marketing (2026)

> Deep-research agent output, 2026 sources. Confidence high on direction; some stats are vendor-report directional.

## Executive summary
The marketer at a 5-50 person startup in 2026 is almost always a generalist — "marketing team of one" / "head of growth" — owning content, social, email, SEO/GEO, paid, analytics at once. AI compressed *drafting* to minutes, but context assembly, fact-checking, voice-matching, review, and distribution did not speed up — so the real bottleneck is context assembly and judgment, not generation. They keep returning to the ChatGPT/Claude blank box despite a crowded field of marketing AI apps, because those apps are siloed, don't know their brand, and force tool-switching. **The workspace they'd love holds their context (brand voice, past posts, product facts) and produces platform-correct output without leaving the page.**

## Who they are
Pre/early-PMF generalist. "Head of Marketing/Growth" or "Founding Marketer," reports to founder, measured on pipeline-adjacent outcomes: activation, signups, conversion, MRR contribution, and increasingly *AI search visibility* (GEO/AEO — brand mentions in ChatGPT/AI Overviews). Fractured day; leans on freelancers for design/deep SEO; perpetually behind.

## What they produce
Each output is platform-shaped — the platform *is* the work:
- **Social** — LinkedIn (carousels top-perform; data-insight posts, polls); X (≤280, punchy, threads); Threads (≤500, calmer, no hashtags). Same idea, three rewrites.
- **Blog** — 800-1,200+ words, expertise-dense, written to be *citable* by AI engines (specific figures, sources).
- **Launch announcements** — Product Hunt copy, launch-day posts, 3-5 email sequence.
- **Email campaigns** — newsletters, nurture, lifecycle.
- **Ad copy** — short, variant-heavy.

## Tools & workflow shift
Solo stack ~$75-150/mo: AI writer + SEO tool + scheduler + CMS + Zapier glue. ~91% of marketing teams use AI daily but only ~23% have it *integrated* — most use it siloed. Drafting collapsed; surrounding steps didn't, so handoffs/review now feel slower by comparison.

## Creation workflow
"Need X" → **gather context first** (past winners, voice rules, product facts, customer quotes, the goal) → draft with AI (~10 min) → **personalize** with specifics only they have → platform-shape → review → schedule. On-screen: AI chat, brand-notes doc, product/site, analytics, target platform. Pain = the context-switching tax (~2.5 hrs/week lost to tab-hopping).

## Good vs. AI-slop
**Slop:** generic openers, hedged advice, no specific data, sounds like every other brand (convergence). **Good:** clear POV, specific figures + sources, real customer/product detail, correct platform shape, on-brand voice. "Done" = specific claim + evidence + their voice + correct format. Review pushes back on: off-brand tone, vague claims, factual errors, missing GEO signals.

## Workspace preferences
Context **on-screen and persistent** — voice pack, banned phrases, past winners, product facts, the goal — never re-pasted. Platform-aware output. Inline editing, no copy-paste. Hate: generic output, tools that don't know their brand, siloed apps, conflicting cross-channel review feedback.

## Design signal for the Studio
Strongest cross-source theme: **context assembly + persistent brand memory + platform-aware output in one place** is the unmet need — maps directly onto BBC's "company brain, role-scoped" thesis. The Marketing Studio already has the platform cards; the gap is the *persistent context on screen* (the sidebar) and keeping review in-place.

# Persona research — Finance / Accounting (2026)

> Deep-research agent output, 2026 sources. New role (not in the original 5).

## Summary
At a 5-50 person startup, finance is one overstretched person — founder/COO early, then a fractional CFO (~$4-10K/mo), then a "Head of Finance" #1 who *builds the function while running it*. Core accountability: **cash truth** — how much runway, why burn moved, what the board should do. **Work product is overwhelmingly written narrative wrapped around numbers — and that narrative layer is the Studio's wedge, not the ledger.**

## What they produce
- **Monthly board financial package** — statements + **2-3 paragraphs of variance commentary** (the deliverable, not the numbers) + 13-week cash forecast + KPI dashboard + agenda naming the 1-2 board decisions.
- **Investor financial updates** — MRR, burn, runway, churn, milestones + narrative outlook.
- **Runway/burn analysis** — runway number + month-by-month forecast showing which months are tight.
- **Fundraising model + narrative** — capital-efficiency framing (burn multiple, CAC payback, Rule of 40).
- **Budget memos, expense policies, monthly close summaries, board-deck financial sections, cap-table comms.**

## Workflow
Gather context in order: actuals (from the close) → prior periods / the plan → assumptions. On screen: accounting system, FP&A model, last month's board deck as template, the budget. Identify *material* variances only, tag each timing-vs-structural, write what/why/what-it-means, reforecast the full year.

## Good vs. mediocre
**Mediocre:** dumps every 2% fluctuation; "revenue 8% below plan" with no cause; numbers without outlook. **Good:** three-part story per material variance — *what happened* / *why* / *what it means* — flags timing vs structural, always connects to updated full-year outlook, includes favorable + unfavorable, concise. "Done" = board can answer "on track? does it matter? new outlook?" without asking.

## Workspace preferences
On-screen: actuals + plan + prior period side by side; last period's deliverable as a reusable template; cash position. Hate: data scattered across systems, line-by-line reconciliation eating the close, "rearview-mirror" reporting, rebuilding the same deck monthly from scratch.

## Design implications for the Finance Studio
- **Wedge = the narrative-around-numbers layer** (variance commentary, investor updates, board memos) — NOT the ledger/modeling.
- Ingest the startup's **own actuals / prior periods / assumptions** as grounding context.
- Default output structure: **what / why / what-it-means + timing-vs-structural** tags.
- **Show its work / sources** — only 14% of CFOs fully trust AI accounting data; 97% insist on human oversight. The Studio earns trust by being auditable and keeping the human as decider.
- Template carries forward month-to-month (same as Founder Studio pattern).
